Introduction

University life is often portrayed as exciting and full of opportunity. However, behind academic achievements lies a growing mental health crisis. This story explores patterns of depression among students and investigates how academic pressure, sleep, social life, and financial stress interact to affect student well-being.

Why This Matters?

Student depression affects not only academic performance but also long-term mental well-being and future career outcomes. Understanding the underlying patterns in student mental health data is essential for designing effective interventions. This interactive story explores how academic, lifestyle, and social factors are associated with depression among students.

Lifestyle vs Depression: Sleep and Diet Comparison

Identify Depression Rates Across Sleep Duration and Dietary Habits

This visualization explores how lifestyle factors, specifically sleep duration and dietary habits, relate to student depression levels. By examining the interactions between these categorical variables, users can identify patterns and potential risk factors associated with depression. The heatmap format allows for a quick comparison across different combinations of sleep and diet, highlighting areas where intervention may be most needed.

Key Insight: Students with shorter sleep durations and unhealthy dietary habits tend to exhibit higher depression levels. Certain combinations, such as adequate sleep with poor diet or insufficient sleep with healthy diet, show moderate depression prevalence, indicating that both factors contribute together.
